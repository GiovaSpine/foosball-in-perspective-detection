Welcome

This web application allows you detects the eight key vertices of a foosball table's play area, allowing you to understand its 3D geometry and leverage it for various tasks, such as translating positions from image coordinates to table coordinates.

The detection is powered by a YOLO-based model, specifically trained on images of foosball matches captured from a spectator's view (see the documentation for details). You can explore the project through the documentation, view the model's detection results, or try it yourself in the Predict section.

In addition to detecting keypoints, the backend also provides APIs for translating points, estimating player line positions, and cleaning keypoint data for improved accuracy (more details in the documentation).

What you can do

  • Learn about the project and its backend APIs in the Documentation section.
  • Check out the model's performance in the Results section, including evaluation metrics and final considerations.
  • Try the model yourself in the Predict section, by uploading your own images or selecting from the sample images provided.
